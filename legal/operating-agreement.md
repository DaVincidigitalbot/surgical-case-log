# OPERATING AGREEMENT OF CLINICAL CASE LOG, LLC

## A North Carolina Limited Liability Company

---

**Effective Date:** March 16, 2026

This Operating Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by the sole Member of Clinical Case Log, LLC ("Company"), a North Carolina limited liability company.

---

## ARTICLE I — FORMATION

**1.1 Formation.** The Company was formed as a North Carolina limited liability company by filing Articles of Organization with the North Carolina Secretary of State pursuant to the North Carolina Limited Liability Company Act (N.C.G.S. Chapter 57D) (the "Act").

**1.2 Name.** The name of the Company is **Clinical Case Log, LLC**.

**1.3 Principal Office.** The principal office of the Company shall be at:

> 202 Keyhole Ct, Cramerton, NC 28032

**1.4 Registered Agent.** The registered agent for service of process is:

> **Graydon Stallard**
> 202 Keyhole Ct, Cramerton, NC 28032

**1.5 Term.** The Company shall continue in existence perpetually unless dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or the Act.

**1.6 Purpose.** The purpose of the Company is to develop, market, sell, and support software products for clinical education and medical case logging, and to engage in any other lawful business activity permitted under the Act.

## ARTICLE II — MEMBER

**2.1 Sole Member.** The sole Member of the Company is:

| Member | Membership Interest |

| **Graydon Stallard** | 100% |

**2.2 Additional Members.** Additional members may be admitted only with the written consent of the existing Member(s) and upon amendment of this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE III — MANAGEMENT

**3.1 Member-Managed.** The Company shall be member-managed. The sole Member shall have full authority to manage the business and affairs of the Company.

**3.2 Powers.** The Member shall have the power to:

- (a) Enter into contracts, agreements, and obligations on behalf of the Company;
- (b) Open and maintain bank accounts and financial accounts;
- (c) Hire employees, contractors, and agents;
- (d) Acquire, hold, and dispose of property;
- (e) Borrow money and incur indebtedness;
- (f) Execute any and all documents necessary to carry out the Company's business;
- (g) Take any other action permitted by the Act.

**3.3 Officers.** The Member may appoint officers (President, Treasurer, Secretary, etc.) to assist in managing the Company. Such officers shall serve at the pleasure of the Member.

---

## ARTICLE IV — CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

**4.1 Initial Contribution.** The sole Member's initial capital contribution is:

| Member | Contribution | Description |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Graydon Stallard | Intellectual property | Software platform "Clinical Case Log Pro," all associated source code, designs, domain names (clinicalcaselog.com), trademarks, and customer relationships |

**4.2 Additional Contributions.** No Member shall be required to make additional capital contributions. Additional contributions may be made at the Member's discretion.

**4.3 No Interest.** No interest shall be paid on capital contributions.

---

## ARTICLE V — DISTRIBUTIONS AND ALLOCATIONS

**5.1 Distributions.** The Member may make distributions of available cash or property at any time and in any amount, at the Member's sole discretion.

**5.2 Allocations.** All profits, losses, and tax items of the Company shall be allocated 100% to the sole Member.

**5.3 Tax Treatment.** The Company shall be treated as a disregarded entity for federal income tax purposes (unless the Member elects otherwise, such as S-Corporation status via IRS Form 2553).

---

## ARTICLE VI — ACCOUNTING AND RECORDS

**6.1 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year.

**6.2 Books and Records.** The Company shall maintain accurate books and records of all transactions, which shall be kept at the principal office.

**6.3 Bank Accounts.** All Company funds shall be deposited in accounts in the Company's name. Company funds shall not be commingled with the personal funds of any Member.

---

## ARTICLE VII — LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

**7.1 Limited Liability.** The Member shall not be personally liable for any debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being a Member.

**7.2 Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Member and any officers or agents from and against any claims, liabilities, damages, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of the good-faith performance of their duties on behalf of the Company, to the fullest extent permitted by the Act.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTEREST

**8.1 Transfer.** The Member may freely transfer all or any portion of their membership interest. Upon transfer of 100% of membership interest, the transferee shall be admitted as a substitute Member upon execution of an amendment to this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE IX — DISSOLUTION

**9.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon:

- (a) The written decision of the Member to dissolve the Company;
- (b) The entry of a judicial decree of dissolution; or
- (c) Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue.

**9.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Member shall wind up the Company's affairs, pay all debts and obligations, and distribute any remaining assets to the Member.

**9.3 Articles of Dissolution.** Upon completion of winding up, Articles of Dissolution shall be filed with the North Carolina Secretary of State.

---

## ARTICLE X — MISCELLANEOUS

**10.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of North Carolina.

**10.2 Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by the Member.

**10.3 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**10.4 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement regarding the Company and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.
